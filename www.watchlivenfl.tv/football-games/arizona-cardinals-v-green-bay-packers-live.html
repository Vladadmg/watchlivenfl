The Green Bay Packers and the Arizona Cardinals will lock horns first in their initial game of the preseason, 2013.Both the teams will fight for a win on Friday, as the Packers host the Cardinals at Lambeau field. The Cardinals do not have much to talk about as far as their player statistics are concerned, but on their day, they are capable of achieving a spectacular win over any sort of opponents.

Although, their rankings suggest otherwise, they are a good side with players, Larry Fitzgerald and Larod Stephen-Howling, playing the key role. The Cardinals need to develop self-confidence, and they can put the Packers on the back foot on Friday. They have a lot of raw talent, and they are expected to become better with time.

Green Bay will be the most affected by injuries, as the better team of the two on paper, and hence Cardinals have a good chance to inflict a blow on the Packers, on Friday. Alex Green will be their go to player in tomorrow’s all important first game of the preseason. An exciting contest is on the cards, as the low performing Cardinals will be up against the Packers without Packers’ key players.

The Green Bay Packers invite the Arizona Cardinals to a Week Nine showdown at Lambeau Field at 1:00PM on Sunday, November 4th. The Green Bay Packers have had a very unfortunate season, with a plethora of injuries affecting their chances of making it into the post season, and it is something that may allow the Cardinals to win against them this Sunday. Green Bay has won three games in a row however, and even with all the injuries if they can prevail against the Cards they will have won their fourth consecutive game.

As for the Cardinals, they are on the opposite end of a streak right now, with four games behind them that were all losses. If they do not beat the understaffed Packers they will have five consecutive losses. With a record on both ends of the spectrum, you'd think it would be easy to pick a winner, but a surprise could be in store. Arizona started out 4-0 at the beginning of the season, but they are a long way from that now, their fourth loss in a row coming from the San Francisco 49ers in Monday night's game.
